Navigating the Future: Trends in Mobile Development

Tuesday, 5.28.24 @11:30am - 12:30pm EST

Location - Virtual Lunch and Learn

About Brooksource

Join us in exploring the dynamic landscape of mobile development as we delve into two key trends that are shaping the future of this ever-evolving field: collaboration and personalization.

First, we'll explore the potential of declarative frameworks, including Jetpack Compose and SwiftUI, and tools for seamless code sharing across platforms and how, with these tools, teams are empowered to move faster and collaborate better by introducing shared patterns and shared logic across Android, iOS, and web platforms.

Then, we'll delve into the transformative impact of AI and ML on the mobile experience. We’ll talk about personalized use cases and the role of tools like ChatGPT. We’ll look at the growing ecosystem of devices and the broader spectrum of problems we can solve on the most suitable devices, contributing to a more comprehensive and adaptive mobile platform.

Join us for this insightful exploration, equipping you with the knowledge to navigate and thrive in the evolving landscape of mobile development.

Meet the presenter: Sierra Obryan, Software Engineer / Clubhouse

Sierra OBryan is a Software Engineer at Clubhouse, who is based in Cincinnati, OH, focused primarily on native Android. With nearly a decade of experience, Sierra has had a varied career after transitioning from academics to tech; she’s worked on platform and feature teams, as a consultant, freelancer and full time across industries, from Fortune 500 companies to start ups to tech giants.

She is passionate about building beautiful and accessible Android apps, sharing knowledge with the community, and mentoring aspiring mobile developers. She is an international speaker, course author, book reviewer, a Google Developer Expert for Android and serves as a Director of Women Who Code Cincinnati, Director of Technology for getWITit (Women in Tech), Women Techmakers global Ambassador, and former global Leadership Fellow for Women Who Code Mobile. She’s been recognized as a Women to Watch Finalist by The Circuit and a Next Generation Leader in Technology by the Northern Kentucky Chamber of Commerce.
