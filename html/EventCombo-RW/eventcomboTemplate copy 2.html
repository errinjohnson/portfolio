Navigating Digital Inclusion

Enhanced User Experience for All:

Accessible design often improves the overall user experience for everyone.

Accessibility design promotes a society where everyone can participate fully, fostering a sense of equality and breaking down barriers for individuals with diverse needs. Join us for an engaging webinar where we'll unravel the secrets to creating apps and websites that everyone can enjoy. Learn the dos and don'ts of accessibility design, discover the power of universal design principles, and master the art of accessibility etiquette. This webinar is not just informative; it's a journey into the exciting realm where technology meets empathy.

Wednesday: January 25th from 11:30-12:30 - zoom link sent after registration

Demonstrating a commitment to accessibility contributes to a positive brand image. Consumers increasingly value businesses that prioritize social responsibility, leading to enhanced brand.

What to Expect:
  • Learn the recommended practices and strategies for creating designs that are accessible to a wide range of users, including those with disabilities.
  • Understand common pitfalls and mistakes to avoid in accessibility design.
  • Recognize elements that may hinder usability for individuals with disabilities and learn how to steer clear of these pitfalls.
  • Hear about advances in technologies that are making accessibility features more feasible and easier to implement.

Accessibility design is not just about meeting legal requirements; it's about creating a world that is open, inclusive, and considerate of the diverse needs of all individuals. It benefits businesses, organizations, and society as a whole, fostering a more equitable and harmonious environment.

Speakers

About Heather

Heather MacKenzie is a technology professional with over 20 years of experience in the field. Throughout her career, Heather has worked in various roles including Software Engineer, Business Analyst, Project Manager, and Program Manager. Currently, she serves as the Director of Global Technology Innovations at the American Printing House for the Blind, where she leads a team responsible for driving innovation across the organization. In addition to her work in technology, Heather is actively involved in Women in Tech groups, where she advocates for gender diversity and promotes opportunities for women in the industry.

About Ken

Ken Perry, a trailblazer in access technology and computer science, discovered his passion for coding with a Commodore Vic-20. Despite a detour from a military career due to blindness, he earned a software engineering degree, shaping his resilient journey. From self-employed Software Engineer to impactful roles at APH, Ken's innovations in accessible technology have left an enduring legacy. A dedicated advocate and educator, he volunteers to empower blind students, contributes to open-source projects, and influences literature for authentic blind representation.
