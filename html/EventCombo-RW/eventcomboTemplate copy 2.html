About Heather

Heather MacKenzie is a technology professional with over 20 years of experience in the field. Throughout her career, Heather has worked in various roles including Software Engineer, Business Analyst, Project Manager, and Program Manager. Currently, she serves as the Director of Global Technology Innovations at the American Printing House for the Blind, where she leads a team responsible for driving innovation across the organization. In addition to her work in technology, Heather is actively involved in Women in Tech groups, where she advocates for gender diversity and promotes opportunities for women in the industry.

About Ken

Ken Perry, a trailblazer in access technology and computer science, discovered his passion for coding with a Commodore Vic-20. Despite a detour from a military career due to blindness, he earned a software engineering degree, shaping his resilient journey. From self-employed Software Engineer to impactful roles at APH, Ken's innovations in accessible technology have left an enduring legacy. A dedicated advocate and educator, he volunteers to empower blind students, contributes to open-source projects, and influences literature for authentic blind representation. Ken's life reflects determination and a commitment to making technology accessible
